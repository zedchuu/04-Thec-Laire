# Chapter 3.1 - 4 Months in

It's been four months since Lily joined the orphanage. I thought she'd be someone I chat with once in a while, I mean she was quiet. Emphasis on was because man was i wrong. I was wrong about most of what I thought she was.

What i found out was.. Lily is a total poser. Genuinely.

She is, right now, beside me as always. The only times she isn't are bedtime and when she's reading. Yeah, when it comes to her books.. i'll lose by a landslide. I tried once. She looked up at me, looked at the page, looked at me again, made faces, and went back to the page. Yup better leave her alone.

The teachers don't bother her anymore. She does her work well and she never causes trouble, so as far as they're concerned she's solved. She went around and asked every teacher to pair her with me for every assignment (where and how do she even got the courage). They agreed of course as they don't care. So now Lily is something I wear. Wherever I go, she goes. Whatever I see, she sees. We're basically the same person, except she's shorter and reads more.

I'm not complaining of course. She's funny.. really funny. She's clumsy in a way I didn't see coming from how careful she looked the first month.. like all that watching never translated into knowing where her feet were. And she's smart also, I mean.. you don't read every day for a month and stay dumb. She's still weird nonetheless. 

With me, she can talk from morning until night. (She has done that. Twice.) Once was about a book where a boy lived inside a clock and I am still not sure I understood the ending. The other time was about whether ants have feelings. She had arguments on both sides. I lost track of which one she was on. She didn't seem to mind. She's just happy to consider both.

The moment a third person walks up, poof. Its almost like the same old lily. But this time she seemed focused and observant instead of disengaged. Of course its funny to me, because I know what she's actually like, and watching her perform "quiet shy girl" when I know she has opinions about ants is one of the funniest things in my life. I wish she'd let other people see it. They'd like her. But thats her choice of course. Im fine with things are. 

The day after we first talked was a snow duty day as mentioned. I was on shoveling. I was working through a big pile near the east wall, half-asleep on my feet, when I heard sobbing.

There was no one around. I checked.

I listened again. The sobbing was coming from inside the pile.

I dug. It was Lily. She had fallen in somehow and could not get out and was, apparently, planning to just stay there. Crying. Quietly. Until what, I don't know. Until spring, maybe.

"Why didn't you call for help?"

She wouldn't look at me. Her face was so red from the cold it looked painted on.

I carried her to the forge. She didn't weigh anything. I sat her by the fire and lectured her the way Miss Elyana would have lectured me, about how you have to ask for things, about how no one can help you if they don't know, about how a person freezing in a snow pile is allowed to make some noise about it. She listened with her whole body, the way she listens to everything.

Then she started crying again. Different crying this time. The first crying was cold-and-stuck. The second one was something else.

She said she was afraid people would find her a burden.

It hit me in a strange place. I knew what she was saying because I had heard a version of it before, from someone else, years ago. Seriph used to talk like that when she was tired. *Don't tell Miss Elyana I'm sad, she has enough to think about.* Miss Elyana would have told her, gently, that being told someone is sad is not a burden, it's a gift, because it means they trusted you.

I didn't have Miss Elyana's words exactly. I had my own, which were clumsier. I told Lily she wasn't a burden. I told her if she ever got stuck in snow again she should yell, and I would come. I told her I had been worried about her for a month before she ever spoke.

She hugged me.

I wasn't ready for it. I didn't know what to do with my arms for a second. She pressed her forehead into my shoulder and made a small sound, like she'd been holding it.

That was four months ago and the problem I have not solved is that she has not really let go.

Not literally. She lets go to sleep, to read, to use her hands. But ever since that day she has decided, without asking anyone including me, that her place in the world is beside me. She walks slightly behind on my left. She eats next to me. She picks the bellows when I'm on hammer and the broom when I'm on dishes. If I turn a corner without telling her, she finds me within a minute. I don't know how. I've tested it.

I should be more bothered by this than I am.

The truth is I like it. I have been at this orphanage for eight years. As mentioned. In eight years I have had three friends. Two died. One was sold. I don't talk about this with anyone because there's no one to talk about it with. Lily showing up and deciding I was hers.. I didn't ask for it, but I'm not going to be the one who hands it back.

So here we are.

She is beside me. She is silent in public, which I have given up on. She is, I am fairly sure, going to be beside me tomorrow, and the day after that, and the day after that.

I'm not going to lose her.

I haven't decided yet if that's a promise or a fact. It feels like both.
